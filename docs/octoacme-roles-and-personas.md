# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile practices, remove impediments, and coach teams to improve collaboration and delivery flow. They ensure sprint ceremonies run effectively and help teams continuously improve.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, standup, review, retrospective)
- Remove blockers and impediments affecting team velocity
- Coach team members on Agile and Scrum practices
- Foster collaboration between team members and across teams
- Shield the team from external distractions
- Track and improve team metrics (velocity, cycle time, etc.)

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and delivery flow
- Reduce blockers and process friction
- Build a high-performing, self-organizing team
- Continuously improve team practices and ceremonies

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-one coaching sessions with team members
- Coordination with Project Manager on delivery coordination
- Collaboration with Product Manager on backlog refinement
- Escalation of organizational impediments

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on overall delivery timeline, dependencies, and resource allocation
- **Product Manager**: Supports backlog refinement and ensures user stories are ready for sprints
- **Developers**: Coaches on Agile practices, removes blockers, facilitates daily execution
- **QA/Testing**: Ensures quality practices are integrated into the sprint workflow
- **DevOps Engineer**: Collaborates on process automation and deployment improvements

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for user needs and design intuitive, accessible experiences. They create and validate UI/UX flows, collaborate on feature requirements, and ensure the product is usable and delightful.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria from a user perspective
- Validate designs with users and iterate based on feedback
- Maintain design systems and consistency across products
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered, intuitive experiences
- Reduce friction and confusion in user workflows
- Ensure accessibility standards are met
- Build cohesive, consistent design language

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and personas
- Wireframes and prototypes shared via design tools
- Collaboration in sprint planning and backlog refinement

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on requirements, user needs, and feature priorities
- **Developer**: Collaborates on implementation feasibility and design handoff
- **QA/Testing**: Validates usability and ensures designs are implemented correctly
- **Technical Writer**: Ensures UI language and help documentation are aligned
- **Business Analyst**: Incorporates user feedback and business requirements into designs

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create, publish, and maintain technical documentation such as release notes, API docs, user guides, and process documentation. They ensure knowledge is accessible and support effective knowledge sharing across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Write and publish API documentation, user guides, and release notes
- Maintain up-to-date technical documentation
- Collaborate with developers on specs and feature documentation
- Review PRs for documentation completeness
- Support internal knowledge sharing and onboarding materials
- Ensure documentation follows style guides and best practices

### Goals
- Make technical information clear and accessible
- Reduce support burden through comprehensive documentation
- Enable faster onboarding and knowledge transfer
- Maintain high documentation quality standards

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with engineering teams
- Collaboration via docs repositories and content management systems
- Participation in release planning to prepare release notes
- Cross-functional coordination on knowledge base updates

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developer**: Reviews code, specs, and technical designs to produce accurate documentation
- **QA/Testing**: Incorporates testing outcomes and known issues into documentation
- **Project Manager**: Documents processes, templates, and project artifacts
- **Product Manager**: Aligns documentation with product messaging and feature rollout
- **UX Designer**: Ensures consistency between UI labels and documentation

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers own the CI/CD pipeline, deployment process, infrastructure automation, and system reliability. They enable fast, safe, and repeatable deployments while ensuring infrastructure scales effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Automate infrastructure provisioning and configuration
- Monitor system health, performance, and security
- Manage deployment process and release coordination
- Implement observability and alerting
- Support incident response and post-incident reviews

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, automated deployments
- Minimize downtime and deployment risks
- Improve system observability and reliability
- Scale infrastructure efficiently

### Typical Communication
- Deployment coordination and release planning
- Incident response and on-call handoffs
- Infrastructure and tooling updates
- Collaboration on performance and reliability improvements

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developer**: Partners on build automation, testing infrastructure, and deployment tooling
- **QA/Testing**: Provides test environments and supports automated testing infrastructure
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on release planning and deployment windows
- **Scrum Master**: Collaborates on process improvements and automation initiatives
- **Technical Writer**: Documents deployment processes and runbooks

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts elicit, document, and analyze requirements to ensure projects deliver business value. They bridge communication between stakeholders and technical teams, clarifying scope and identifying gaps.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Support stakeholder alignment and requirement prioritization
- Create process flows, use cases, and requirement specifications
- Facilitate requirements workshops and reviews
- Track requirement changes and maintain traceability

### Goals
- Ensure clear, actionable requirements
- Align technical solutions with business objectives
- Reduce scope ambiguity and rework
- Support effective stakeholder communication

### Typical Communication
- Requirement documents and business process diagrams
- Stakeholder workshops and requirement reviews
- Collaboration with PM and PdM on scope and priorities
- Status updates on requirement completeness

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Works closely on requirements grooming and backlog prioritization
- **Project Manager**: Supports scope definition, gap analysis, and change management
- **Stakeholders**: Gathers feedback, clarifies needs, and validates solutions
- **Developer**: Provides detailed requirements and clarifies acceptance criteria
- **QA/Testing**: Collaborates on test scenarios based on business requirements
